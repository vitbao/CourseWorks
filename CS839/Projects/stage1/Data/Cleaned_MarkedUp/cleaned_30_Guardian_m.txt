 The British singer has recorded a song for the soundtrack of <pname>Ava DuVernay</pname>'s film adaptation of A Wrinkle in Time <pname>Laura Snapes</pname>  Wed 21 Feb 2018 06.43 EST   Last modified on Wed 21 Feb 2018 06.44 EST  <pname>Sade</pname> has recorded a new original song for the upcoming film adaptation of A Wrinkle in Time. Director <pname>Ava DuVernay</pname> announced the news on her Twitter account: “I never thought she'd say yes, but asked anyway. She was kind + giving. A goddess. We began a journey together that I'll never forget.”Titled Flower of the Universe, the song will be the first new music from Sade since her eponymous band's 2010 album Soldier of Love, their sixth. The soundtrack to A Wrinkle in Time will also feature music from artists including DJ <pname>Khaled</pname> and <pname>Demi Lovato</pname>, <pname>Sia</pname>, <pname>Kehlani</pname> and <pname>Beyoncé</pname> proteges <pname>Chloe</pname> x <pname>Halle</pname>. <Iranian-German composer Ramin Djawadi – best known for writing the score to Game of Thrones – will provide original composition.I never thought she'd say yes, but asked anyway. It's entitled “Flower of the Universe.” And it's a dream come true. pic.twitter.com/FdXrZ1MFMO <pname>Sade</pname>, 59, was born <pname>Helen Folasade Adu</pname> in Ibadan, Nigeria. In 1983, she left the band Pride, in which she was a backing singer, to form her own band with guitarist and saxophone player <pname>Stuart Matthewman</pname>, keyboardist <pname>Andrew Hale</pname>, bassist <pname>Paul Denman</pname> and drummer <pname>Paul Anthony Cook</pname>. Their debut single, Your Love Is King, was released in February 1984, followed by their debut album, Diamond Life, that July. The album has been certified four times platinum. In the Queen's 2017 birthday honours list, <pname>Sade</pname> was awarded a CBE for services to music.A Wrinkle in Time is released on 9 March, and stars <pname>Oprah Winfrey</pname>, <pname>Reese Witherspoon</pname>, <pname>Mindy Kaling</pname> and <pname>Gugu Mbatha-Raw</pname>. <pname>DuVernay</pname> was announced as director of the film in February 2016, making her the first woman of colour to direct a live-action film with a production budget of more than $100m.
